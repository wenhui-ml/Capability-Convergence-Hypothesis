\section{First Controlled Evidence at Small Scale}
\label{sec:experiments}

The predictions of Section~\ref{sec:predictions} were published as schematic
figures; this section reports the first controlled measurements of
Predictions~\ref{pred:floor}--\ref{pred:comm}, together with a model-level
dissociation experiment linking CCH to PRH. Everything here operates strictly
inside the regime $\Rgm$ the paper is scoped to: fixed depth, no
chain-of-thought, no tools, fixed per-token compute; within each comparison only
the sequence-mixing operator differs, with identical data distribution,
optimizer, and training budget. We emphasize the scale up front: the trained
models are $\sim$0.2--1.1M parameters and the public-checkpoint analysis stops
at $\sim$3B. These are small-scale existence tests of the predicted
\emph{mechanisms}, not demonstrations at frontier scale
(cf.\ Limitation~(v), Section~\ref{sec:limitations}).

\paragraph{Pre-registration protocol.}
All support/falsification criteria were frozen in a written protocol
(\texttt{experiments/PREREGISTRATION.md}, dated 2026-07-10) before the
corresponding data were collected: pass thresholds (e.g.\ accuracy $\ge 0.9$;
a cell passes if $\ge 2/3$ seeds pass), the single fitted constant permitted in
Experiment~B ($p_{\mathrm{eff}}$, fit once on one architecture and frozen for
the others), and the explicit falsification conditions for each prediction.
Six amendments were logged with timestamps before the corresponding data
existed (a depth-advantaged Transformer control; a local-window hybrid control;
a $5\times$ budget extension with decision thresholds unchanged, arranged so
that within every comparative claim the predicted-fail side received at least
the budget of the predicted-pass side; a switch to dense LM supervision applied
uniformly to all architectures; and two redesigns of confounded conditions,
each reported below alongside the failed-as-designed original). All completed
cells are reported; no seeds or cells were excluded post hoc. Code, seeds, raw
result files, and the protocol are included in the repository for reproduction.

\subsection{P2, measured: the state-tracking bifurcation (Experiment C)}
\label{sec:expC}

\textbf{Setup.} $S_5$ word problem: the label at position $t$ is the running
product $g_1\!\circ\!\cdots\!\circ\! g_t$ (120-way classification). Two
streams: \emph{swap} (each token a transposition) and \emph{general} (each
token an arbitrary $S_5$ element). Training length $T\!\le\!40$; evaluation at
$T\in\{40,64,128,256,512\}$; ``length-generalizes'' $=$ pass at $T{=}256$
($6.4\times$ training length). Architectures (2 layers, $d_{\mathrm{model}}
{=}128$, 3 seeds): DeltaNet with $\beta\in(0,1)$ (no reflection --- the
production parameterization, the starred prediction), DeltaNet
$\beta\in(0,2)$, DeltaProduct $n_h\in\{2,4\}$~\citep{grazzi2025deltaproduct},
diagonal SSM, Transformer (2-layer, and a depth-advantaged 8-layer with
$4\times$ the parameters), and LSTM.

\textbf{Results (Figure~\ref{fig:nhgrid}).} All six pre-registered predictions
held.
(i)~\emph{The starred prediction:} DeltaNet $\beta\in(0,1)$ fails both streams
at $T{=}256$ --- in fact it stays at chance ($\approx 0.008$) even at the
training length after a $5\times$ budget extension (100k steps): without
reflections the model cannot even \emph{fit} composed $S_5$, a stronger outcome
than predicted.
(ii)~Unlocking one reflection ($\beta\in(0,2)$, $n_h{=}1$) is alone sufficient
for the swap stream: accuracy $1.0/1.0/0.998$ across seeds at $T{=}256$.
(iii)~On the general stream the expressivity ladder appears at $n_h{=}4$:
accuracy at $T{=}256$ is $0.010$ ($n_h{=}1$), $0.011$ ($n_h{=}2$), and
$\{0.995, 1.000, 0.013\}$ across seeds ($n_h{=}4$; 2/3 pass, one seed never
converged), with one $n_h{=}4$ seed extrapolating perfectly to $T{=}512$
($12.8\times$ training length). The measured transition is a step rather than a
graded staircase --- $n_h{=}2$ gains nothing --- which satisfies the
pre-registered monotonicity-plus-endpoints criterion but is sharper than the
schematic grid suggested.
(iv)~Both Transformers fail extrapolation: the 2-layer control is at chance
everywhere (consistent with fixed-depth $\mathrm{TC}^0$ limits,
Proposition~\ref{prop:circuit}); the 8-layer control reaches $1.0$ in-length on
swap and collapses to $0.03$ at $T{=}64$ --- high in-distribution performance
with immediate extrapolation failure, the exact signature the circuit wall
predicts for a fixed-depth parallel architecture.
(v)~The diagonal SSM is at chance in every cell.
(vi)~The LSTM (nonlinear full-rank recurrence) passes everything: accuracy
$1.000$ at every length on both streams, including $T{=}512$.
An honest optimization note: at the original 20k-step budget the LSTM
(general stream) and DeltaProduct $n_h{=}4$ had not yet converged; the
conclusion-relevant budget extension was pre-registered before those runs, and
every predicted-fail cell received at least the budget of every predicted-pass
cell, so no failure above is attributable to budget.

\subsection{P1, measured: the information floor and the scissors gap
(Experiment B)}
\label{sec:expB}

\textbf{Setup.} Executable Newton's-apple task $\mathrm{NA}(N,b,B;\kappa)$:
$N$ key--value bindings ($b{=}8$ bits per value) planted in a length-512
stream with semantic-overlap distractors $\kappa$, single query at the end.
Pure-state models (DeltaNet) with total recurrent state $m\in\{64,256,1024\}$
scalars; sliding-window attention ($w{=}32$); a hybrid holding the \emph{same}
$m{=}64$ state plus one global-attention layer; the same state plus a
window-only layer (not access-complete); and full attention. 3 seeds
throughout; identical budgets.

\textbf{Results (Figure~\ref{fig:fano}).}
(i)~\emph{Floor rise:} every pure-state error curve rises monotonically in $N$
and saturates toward the $b$-bit chance floor (error $0.994/0.977/0.854$ at
$N{=}128$ for $m{=}64/256/1024$; chance $0.996$).
(ii)~\emph{Load-ratio collapse:} with the single frozen constant
$p_{\mathrm{eff}}{=}0.39$ bits/scalar (fit on $m{=}256$ only), the 50\%-error
crossings of all three pure-state curves land at load ratio
$x = Nb/(m\,p_{\mathrm{eff}}) \in [0.82, 1.03]$, and the $m{=}256$ and
$m{=}1024$ curves collapse onto each other within $0.024$ error at shared $x$.
The pre-registered $\pm 0.1$ collapse band is met by the two larger states but
violated (deviation up to $0.15$) by $m{=}64$, whose runs sit at the edge of
trainability (see below); we report the criterion as only partially met.
(iii)~\emph{The scissors gap, measured:} at $N{=}128$ the pure $m{=}64$ model
has error $0.994$ while the hybrid carrying the \emph{same} 64-scalar state
plus one global-attention layer has mean error $0.060$
(seeds $0.018/0.125/0.038$) --- the measured counterpart of
Figure~\ref{fig:fano}'s predicted dive, though the strict pre-registered bound
($\le 0.05$ at every $N$) is crossed at the last grid point by one seed.
Its conditional log-loss rides near zero while every pure-state curve tracks
the $b(1-1/x)$ floor of Theorem~\ref{thm:shannon}.
(iv)~\emph{The index must be global:} the same state paired with a
window-limited layer is not rescued (error $\ge 0.91$ at all $N$) --- being
access-complete, not merely having attention, is what matters
(cf.\ Figure~\ref{fig:sankey}).
(v)~\emph{Horizon wall:} the sliding-window model is at chance ($0.99+$) for
bindings beyond its window at \emph{every} $N$; trained only on far-binding
layouts it never learns retrieval at all. Within-window retrieval is good at
small $N$ (error $0.07$ at $N{=}2$) but the pre-registered
``near-perfect independent of $N$'' clause is not realizable in this design
(more than $\sim$8 bindings no longer fit inside the window); we count the
prediction as only partially confirmed.
(vi)~\emph{Fano sanity:} no architecture beats the floor computed from nominal
capacity anywhere on the grid.
(vii)~\emph{Compressibility:} with values duplicated in blocks ($c{=}16$) the
error wall shifts right to a 50\%-crossing at $N\!\approx\!27$, matching the
effective-entropy prediction ($N\!\approx\!25$) rather than the nominal one
($N\!\approx\!12$); the intermediate $c{=}4$ condition, however, tracks the
nominal prediction, and the block design admits a retrieval-free shortcut at
$N\le c$, so we report this axis as mixed evidence.
(viii)~\emph{A null and an anomaly, reported as such:} the semantic-overlap
ordering ($\kappa$) shows \emph{no} detectable effect at matched weights
(mean error $0.610$ vs.\ $0.609$); and small 2-layer \emph{full attention} is
not the upper-bound reference the schematic assumed --- its error climbs to
$0.91$ at $N{=}128$, far above the hybrid's, an optimization (not capacity)
failure at this scale. The access-complete witness in these experiments is the
hybrid, exactly the pattern R1--R3 (Section~\ref{sec:regularities}) document in
production models. Finally, trainability itself depended on state size: 3 of 6
pure $m{=}64$ runs never learned even $N{=}2$ (no such failure at
$m\in\{256,1024\}$ or for any hybrid); all reported means include these seeds,
per protocol.

\subsection{Representational convergence without capability convergence
(Experiment A)}
\label{sec:expA}

\textbf{Setup.} Three public model families trained on the same corpus (The
Pile) with the same tokenizer, spanning 70M--3B: Pythia (attention), Mamba
(pure SSM), RWKV-4 (linear RNN). Capability axis: $N$ templated facts planted
at controlled depth in Pile-style filler ($L\in\{896,1900\}$), exact-match
retrieval at the query. Representation axis: PRH's own metric --- mutual
$k$-NN alignment ($k{=}10$; CKA as a check) between all model pairs on 1024
shared Pile snippets, max over layer pairs.

\textbf{Results (Figure~\ref{fig:dissoc}).}
(i)~\emph{PRH replicates on this triplet:} cross-family representational
alignment grows with scale (Spearman $\rho = 0.57/0.87/0.51$ for
Pythia--Mamba/Pythia--RWKV/Mamba--RWKV; $n=30/24/20$ pairs, all $p<0.05$),
reaching $0.7+$ against a chance level of $0.01$ --- the first convergence, as
reported by~\citet{huh2024platonic}, is real here.
(ii)~\emph{Capability nonetheless stratifies by access structure:} at matched
$\sim$2.8B scale ($N{=}16$ early-depth facts, $L{=}1900$), retrieval accuracy
is Pythia $0.35$, Mamba $0.08$, RWKV $0.00$. The pre-registered margin
($\ge 30$pp over both recurrent families) is met against RWKV ($35$pp) but
narrowly missed against Mamba ($27$pp); the ordering
Pythia $>$ Mamba $>$ RWKV holds in every matched-scale cell at $N\ge 16$.
(iii)~\emph{The failure signature is architectural:} Mamba's accuracy falls as
facts move away from the query (recency; e.g.\ $0.06\!\to\!0.37$ mean accuracy
from early to late depth for Mamba-1.4b) while Pythia-2.8b is nearly flat
($0.44\!\to\!0.48$); RWKV sits at an accuracy floor at all depths.
(iv)~\emph{The dissociation:} across 74 cross-family pairs, representational
alignment does \emph{not} predict capability parity --- the correlation
between alignment and the absolute retrieval gap is significantly
\emph{positive} (Spearman $\rho = +0.48$, $p<10^{-4}$; the pre-registered
falsification was a significantly negative one), and the
high-alignment/large-gap quadrant is well populated. Representational
convergence, PRH's endpoint, does not purchase capability convergence; the
capability boundary tracks the access structure. This is the measured form of
the paper's central claim that the second convergence is a distinct, physical
one.

\subsection{P3 miniature: a failed prediction, reported as such
(Experiment D)}
\label{sec:expD}

We tested Prediction~\ref{pred:comm} in miniature on the trained hybrids of
Experiment~B: mutual $k$-NN alignment between the state-channel and
index-channel layer outputs at matched positions, for task-solving final
checkpoints vs.\ failing (undertrained and degenerate) checkpoints vs.\ random
initialization. \textbf{The prediction failed, with the direction reversed:}
solving hybrids show the \emph{lowest} cross-channel alignment
($0.28$ mean, $n{=}9$), below failing checkpoints ($0.38$--$0.53$) and below
random initialization ($0.58$, $n{=}3$). Two lessons, stated plainly. First,
the schematic premise that random initialization is a low-alignment floor is
empirically wrong for this metric: two randomly initialized channels reading
the same tokens are already highly $k$-NN-aligned, because both echo input
geometry. Second, in hybrids that solve the task, training \emph{differentiates}
the channels rather than aligning them --- consistent with the two channels
carrying complementary loads (compressive shortlist vs.\ verbatim index), which
is the mechanism of Section~\ref{sec:cross}, but the opposite of the
commensurability signature Prediction~\ref{pred:comm} registered. As committed
in the protocol, we report this as a failed prediction of CCH:
Prediction~\ref{pred:comm} as operationalized (alignment above a random floor)
must be retracted or re-operationalized --- a metric of channel
\emph{complementarity} rather than channel \emph{similarity} --- and
Figure~\ref{fig:comm} retains its schematic label with this negative result
noted in its caption.

\subsection{Deviations, and what these results do not establish}
\label{sec:exp-honesty}

\textbf{Deviations from the frozen protocol} (beyond the six timestamped
amendments): the sliding-window width was $w{=}32$, not the registered
$w{=}64$ (the receptive field remains far smaller than the binding--query
distance, which is what the horizon-wall logic requires); Experiment~A used
$L\in\{896,1900\}$ rather than $2048$ and $100$ rather than $\ge 200$ instances
per cell (context-window and cost constraints of the public checkpoints); the
exact analytic entropy of the compressibility conditions was approximated by
$b-\log_2 c$; the $\beta\in(0,1)$-hybrid arm of Experiment~D was not run.
None of these was decided after seeing results they could affect.

\textbf{Scorecard.} Of 19 pre-registered predictions: 11 supported, 7 partial
(direction confirmed, a frozen threshold or sub-clause missed --- each
detailed above with the miss stated), 1 failed (D-i, direction reversed).
None of the pre-registered \emph{falsification} conditions for P1 or P2 was
triggered: no pure-state architecture held low error at high load, no
$\beta\in(0,1)$ model length-generalized composed $S_5$, no diagonal SSM
passed, and the $n_h$ monotonicity was not violated.

\textbf{Scope.} These experiments establish that the predicted mechanisms ---
the information floor, the scissors gap, the reflection-gated bifurcation, and
the dissociation of representational from capability convergence --- are real
and land where CCH says they land, at small scale, under gradient descent
rather than by construction (addressing Limitation~(iv)). They do not
establish the magnitudes at frontier scale (Limitation~(v) stands), they
leave P3 without positive evidence, and two comparisons (the Pythia--Mamba
margin; the $\pm 0.1$ collapse band at $m{=}64$) missed their frozen
thresholds and are claimed only as directional.
